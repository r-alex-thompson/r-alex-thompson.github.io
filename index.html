About

I'm a data scientist with a background in plant physiological ecology.

In my current role as an NSF Graduate Research Fellow, I am responsible for applying complex statistical analyses to experimental data from a long-term drought experiment to build predictive models of tree mortality.

I have leveraged mathematical modeling techniques from dynamical systems and stochastic processes to model tree responses to the environment, explored spatial patterns of tree mortality in response to climate, and have extensive experience visualizing the results of these models using Python and R.

I'm always interested in collaborating on fun data projects. Feel free to reach out at robert.alex.thompson@gmail.com
